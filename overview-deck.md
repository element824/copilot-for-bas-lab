# GitHub Copilot for **Business Analysts**

## A 90-minute hands-on lab

Build your own GitHub project space, write a structured spec, refine it with Copilot, and publish it — **without writing a single line of code.**

<br>

**Facilitator:** Kousik Nagarajan · Microsoft
**Audience:** Business Analysts
**Format:** Hands-on · Bring your own laptop

---

# Why we're here

Your **BAs live in Confluence.** Your **devs live in GitHub / ADO.**

In between, requirements get **lost in translation** — copied to Teams, summarised into tickets, paraphrased in PRs.

> The cost isn't measured in hours. It's measured in **rework, defects, and systems that don't behave the way you asked.**

Today we build a **new home for requirements** where:

- ✏️ You still write the way you always have — prose, headings, tables, diagrams
- 🔗 The spec **lives next to the code** that implements it
- 🤖 **Copilot is your co-author** — not a developer's tool

---

# The 90-minute arc

| # | Section | Time | What you'll do |
|---|---|:--:|---|
| 0 | Overview & fork the repo | 10 | Get your own forked project space |
| 1 | Project spaces | 15 | Create Project board + first Issue |
| 2 | Structured specs (spec kit) | 15 | Write Overview + Persona by hand |
| 3 | **Refine with Copilot** ⭐ | 20 | Prose → Gherkin in one prompt |
| 4 | Change management | 15 | Draft → page review → publish |
| 5 | End-to-end trace | 10 | Issue ↔ Spec ↔ PR ↔ board |
| — | Q&A + pick the trial mini-project | 5 | Take it home |

# How this works · what you take home

## Ground rules

- 🪪 **You drive your own keyboard.** Don't watch — *do*.
- 👍 **React in chat** at every checkpoint so the room moves together.
- ✋ **Stop me whenever it stops looking like Confluence** — that's the whole point.
- 🆘 **Stuck?** Drop a message in the lab chat. See `STUCK.md`.

## You'll leave with

- ✅ Your **own forked repo** — keep it, share it, start using it Monday
- ✅ A **2-week trial plan** picked from this room
- ✅ Re-usable **templates and Copilot prompts**
