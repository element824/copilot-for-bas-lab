# Pitch — opening for the lab (≈ 90 seconds, spoken)

> "Before we open anything, let me tell you what we're actually doing for the next 90 minutes.
>
> You live in Confluence. Your devs live in GitHub or ADO. And somewhere in between, **requirements get lost in translation** — copied into a Teams chat, summarised in a ticket, paraphrased in a PR description. By the time code ships, nobody is quite sure which version of the requirement was built.
>
> The cost of that gap isn't measured in hours. **It's measured in rework, defects, and the system not behaving the way you asked.**
>
> So today is not a tools demo. Today is a **hands-on lab where you build a new home for requirements** — one where:
>
> - You still write the way you always have: prose, headings, tables, diagrams.
> - The spec lives **next to the code it describes**, so a change in one is visible in the other.
> - And an **AI co-author** sits beside you, turning narrative into acceptance criteria, finding inconsistencies, and answering *'where is this defined?'* in seconds.
>
> By the end of the next 90 minutes, three things will be true:
>
> 1. You'll each have **your own forked project space** with a Project board, Issue, spec and pull request you built yourself.
> 2. You'll have **felt the BA workflow** — write a story, refine it, get acceptance criteria, link it to code — without writing a line of code.
> 3. And you'll know exactly what a **2-week trial** would look like, with a mini-project picked from this room.
>
> One ground rule: **stop me whenever it stops looking like Confluence.** That's the whole point — if any moment feels like 'developer territory', call it out and we'll work out how to hide it.
>
> Right — let's open the wiki."

---

## The 3 messages to land

1. **This is a wiki, not an IDE.** Lower the cognitive barrier first.
2. **Specs and code share a home.** That's the structural shift Confluence cannot give them.
3. **Copilot is the BA's co-author, not the dev's tool.** Reframe who the AI serves.

---

## The closing line (~ 30 seconds, after the lab)

> "Three things changed in the last 90 minutes that didn't change in the last 15 years of Confluence:
>
> - Your spec **knows what the code is doing**.
> - Your acceptance criteria are **one prompt away**, not one workshop away.
> - And every change is **reviewable, traceable and reversible** — without anyone having to remember to copy it anywhere.
>
> The trial isn't about replacing Confluence next month. It's about picking one mini-project, running it here for two weeks, and letting your verdict — not ours — decide whether this is lighter or heavier than what you do today.
>
> You'll keep your fork. Take it back to your team and start Monday."
